How we screen

Two-tier certification:

Industry exclusions (never appear in the universe):

  • Conventional banks (JPM, BAC, WFC, GS, MS…), insurance (AIG, MET, PRU…), credit-card lenders (AXP, DFS, SYF)
  • Alcohol (BUD, STZ, DEO, TAP), tobacco (MO, PM, BTI)
  • Gambling (WYNN, LVS, MGM, DKNG, PENN, CZR), weapons (LMT, RTX, NOC, GD)

What you won't see:

  • Options — calls, puts, vertical spreads suppressed (AAOIFI Sharia Standard 20: gharar + maysir).
  • Short-selling tactics — bay' al-ma'dum + interest-based margin borrow. DOWN signals are reframed as "avoid this position / wait for reversal."

Dividend purification (optional / debated): some AAOIFI-aligned scholars require donating the small slice of dividends that comes from non-compliant income (~1-5%) to remove "tainted" earnings. Other rulings argue that if a stock passes the compliance screen, no purification is needed. For SPUS/HLAL holdings, the fund itself already applies purification before distributing — you don't need to do it personally. We don't surface this on every card because it's not universally practiced and the % depends on the specific stock. If you want exact purification ratios, check Zoya.

The workflow runs 4 times per US weekday (14:30, 17:00, 19:30, 22:00 UTC) plus on manual trigger. The 22:00 UTC run is authoritative (uses the closing bar); intraday runs include partial bars and current prices.

💸 Transaction cost reality check: The K-fold accuracy numbers above are raw — they don't subtract the bid-ask spread, commissions, and slippage you pay on each trade. Realistic round-trip costs on liquid US stocks are 0.05–0.3%. A 55% raw accuracy on 5-day trades with a 1% directional threshold effectively becomes ~51-53% after costs. Always assume your real edge is 2-4pp lower than the headline number when deciding whether to actually trade a signal.

What do these numbers mean? (read this first)
  • Pattern meta-ensemble (K-fold) — combines methodologies, K-fold out-of-sample. Caveat: methodologies share patterns so votes are partially correlated. Bearish meta signals are auto-suppressed when their backtest accuracy is < 45% (markets drift up; bearish meta has been ~29% accurate — the numerical model handles bearish instead).
  • Numerical model (K-fold) — pure logistic regression on continuous features. Independent of pattern recognition. Currently the strongest single source — including for bearish predictions.
  • Consensus families — decorrelated: 6 INDEPENDENT pattern families (trend, momentum, volatility, volume, candlestick, RS), each casts ONE vote. Bayesian log-odds calibration with 0.95 confidence cap.
  • Confidence = expected hit rate (Bayesian-calibrated). Hard cap at 0.95. Consensus = how unanimously voters agree (separate metric — could be 100% consensus with only 60% confidence if voters have modest accuracy).
  • In-sample meta — same combiner, evaluated on training data. Optimistic and biased upward.
  • All-patterns ensemble (in Details below) — naive baseline. Includes noisy short horizons.

📖 Glossary — what every badge, label, and number means

Every visual element used elsewhere on the dashboard, with its actual rendering shown alongside the meaning. Match what you see on a recommendation card to a row here.

Direction tags

UP
Bullish prediction — the model expects the price to rise over the chosen horizon. Action: go long (buy shares) or buy a call option.
DOWN
Bearish prediction — the model expects the price to fall. Action: short the stock (requires margin), buy a put option, or simply don't buy.
—
Neutral / no directional call — patterns are mixed or too weak to lean either way. The system isn't recommending an action.

Recommendation source badges

decorrelated
Decorrelated families consensus — the strongest source. At least 3 INDEPENDENT pattern families (trend, momentum, volatility, volume, candlestick, relative-strength) agreed on the direction. Bayesian-calibrated confidence, capped at 0.95.
correlated meta
Correlated meta-ensemble — older approach. Multiple methodologies agreed, but those methodologies share patterns (e.g., trend-following and trend-in-regime both include SMA crossovers), so the agreement is partially redundant. Lower trust than decorrelated.
tentative · all-ensemble only
Tentative — neither meta nor consensus families fired, but the raw all-pattern ensemble has high conviction (≥0.75 confidence, ≥3 patterns fired). Worth considering but unvalidated by rigorous methodology. Treat as a weaker signal.
no actionable signal
No signal today — patterns are too few, too mixed, or too weak. The ticker is shown so you can still search/check it, but no recommendation. The card displays an "all-ensemble hint" with the raw direction if any.

Numbers on a recommendation card

conf 0.738
Confidence = expected hit rate, calibrated 0.50 (no info) → 0.95 (hard cap, never claims certainty). A 0.74 means the system expects to be right about 74% of the time on signals like this one. Bayesian log-odds calibration for decorrelated; capped at 0.95.
consensus 87.5%
Consensus = how unanimously the voting methodologies/families agreed. 0% = tied, 100% = unanimous. NOT a price-change prediction. A 100% consensus on DOWN means "everyone voted bearish," not "expects 100% price decline."
3 families agree
Voter count — number of independent pattern families (or methodologies, for the correlated meta tier) that fired the same direction. More agreement = more conviction.
5 patterns fired
Pattern count (tentative cards only) — number of individual technical patterns that fired in the same direction. Replaces "consensus" labeling for the tentative tier since no methodology breakdown is available.
#1
Rank — position within the current filtered list, sorted by confidence. #1 is your highest-conviction pick under the current filters.

Volatility matches your risk profile. ATR (Average True Range) as % of price falls in the 2–5% band ("mid-vol"). Match score is the bell-curve distance from your target ATR (set by Risk per trade %).

high-vol (vol-match 25%)
Volatility mismatch with your risk profile. E.g., a meme stock at 9% ATR when you've set 1% risk. The stock isn't excluded but is heavily de-prioritized in ranking.

News sentiment chips

news bullish
Recent news is positive for this ticker. VADER sentiment analysis on Yahoo Finance headlines scored ≥ +0.25 (asymmetric threshold to compensate for VADER's known positive bias on financial text).
news bearish
Recent news is negative. Score ≤ -0.02 (lower threshold because VADER under-detects negative financial language).
news neutral
Recent news doesn't lean strongly either way. Mixed or routine coverage.
↑ vs peers
News is bullish relative to today's universe. This ticker's sentiment score is more than 0.75 standard deviations above the median across all watchlist tickers today. Useful for catching mild positives that the absolute threshold missed.
↓ vs peers
News is bearish relative to peers. Below the universe median by ≥ 0.75σ. Often surfaces real negative signal that VADER's absolute scoring under-rates.
⚠ news disagrees
Sentiment contradicts the model. E.g. model says DOWN but news is bullish. Either the news is noise (boilerplate upgrades after a run-up) or the model is wrong. Worth investigating before trading.
✓ news agrees
Sentiment confirms the model. News and model point the same direction — extra confirmation.

Event risk warnings

⚠ earnings in 4d
Earnings event before the horizon closes. Earnings reports cause large outsized moves unrelated to technical setup. The system flags this so you can avoid being caught by an earnings surprise. Personal Agent has a "Skip earnings" filter (on by default).
earnings in 32d (after horizon)
Earnings exist but after the prediction horizon. Position should be closed before earnings, so it's informational only — no warning.

Market regime tags (in tables / scoreboards)

bull
Bull market regime. SPY is above its 200-day SMA AND the 200-day SMA is rising over the last 60 bars. Trend-following methods work best here.
bear
Bear market regime. SPY is below its 200-day SMA AND falling. Trend-following on the short side works; mean reversion is choppy.
choppy
Choppy / transitional regime. SPY is near or whipsawing around its 200-SMA. Mean reversion sometimes works; trend-following generally doesn't. The decorrelated families method handles this regime best.

Position & risk concepts

Long / Short
Long = buy shares, profit if price rises. Short = borrow + sell, profit if price falls. Shorting requires a margin account; not allowed in IRAs/Roth.
Stop-loss
Pre-defined exit price that limits loss. Set at 2× the 14-day ATR (Average True Range) below entry for longs / above entry for shorts. If price hits stop, the position closes automatically.
ATR
Average True Range — a 14-day measure of typical daily price swing. Used to size stop-losses (volatile stocks get wider stops).
Risk per trade %
Maximum % of your capital you're willing to lose on a single trade if the stop hits. 2% is the standard recommendation. The agent calculates share count to hit this risk ceiling exactly.
Max position %
Maximum % of capital in one position. Caps concentration regardless of how the stop math worked out. 25% is reasonable for a 4-position portfolio.

Options concepts

Long call / long put
Single-leg options. Long call = bet price rises (limited downside = premium paid, unlimited upside). Long put = bet price falls.
Bull call spread
Two-leg debit spread. Buy a call at strike A, sell a call at strike B (B > A). Cheaper than a long call but caps your max profit at (B − A − net debit). Best for moderate bullish conviction.
Bear put spread
The bearish mirror image. Buy a put at strike B, sell a put at strike A (A < B). Limited risk, capped profit, for moderate bearish conviction.
DTE (Days To Expiration)
How many calendar days until the option expires. Standard retail directional plays use 30–90 DTE. The system targets `horizon + 30` days (clamped to 45–365). Options aren't recommended for horizons >1 year (LEAPS territory, illiquid).
Strike
The price at which the option can be exercised. Long strike = the option you buy. Short strike = the option you sell (in a spread). System rounds to standard strike grids ($1 under $50, $2.50 under $200, $5 above).

Pattern families

Trend
Trend-following patterns — SMA crossovers (20/50, 50/200 = golden/death cross), MACD crossovers, multi-timeframe alignment (price > 5/20/50/200 SMA stack), Ichimoku Tenkan-Kijun cross.
Momentum
Oscillator extremes — RSI(14) oversold (<30) or overbought (>70) and turning, Stochastic %K crossing %D from extremes.
Volatility
Bollinger Band squeezes — when bandwidth contracts (low volatility) and then price breaks out. Squeeze + breakout up = bullish, squeeze + breakout down = bearish.
Volume
Volume conviction patterns — high-volume breakouts (z-score > 2), volume dry-up at new lows (selling exhaustion), volume dry-top at new highs (rally without conviction).
Candlestick
Single- and two-candle reversal patterns — bullish/bearish engulfing, hammer, shooting star, doji at support/resistance.
Relative strength
Out/under-performance vs SPY over 20 days. RS bull = ticker beat SPY by ≥3%; RS bear = ticker underperformed by ≥3%.

Backtest accuracy metrics

K-fold accuracy
Out-of-sample accuracy from 5-fold cross-validation. The model is trained on 4 folds, tested on the held-out fold; repeated 5 times. This is the honest "expected real-world hit rate." Trust this number.
Meta + sector weighting (K-fold)
Sector-aware K-fold accuracy — same protocol as plain K-fold but methodology weights are computed per-(sector, methodology) on each training fold. Tests whether sector-specific weighting actually improves out-of-sample accuracy. The delta line shows the lift vs the no-sector baseline; if positive (green), sector weighting helps. Currently the delta is small or slightly negative — sector samples per cell aren't large enough for the weighting to outperform global; sectors are most useful for filtering (e.g., skipping Utilities entirely) rather than as fine-grained weights.
In-sample accuracy
Accuracy on the training data. Always inflated vs out-of-sample. Shown only for comparison; do not act on this number.
PRUNED
Methodology pruned — its backtest accuracy is below 50% at every horizon, meaning it's net-harmful. Excluded from the meta-ensemble until it improves.

How it works (patterns + windows + sources)

Short-term (5–14 days)

  • RSI(14), 5-day SMA
  • Candlestick: engulfing, hammer, shooting star, doji
  • Volume breakouts (20-bar z-score)
  • ATR(14) for stop-loss

Medium-term (20–50 days)

  • 20/50 SMA, Bollinger Bands (60-bar squeeze)
  • MACD(12/26/9)
  • Relative strength vs SPY (20d)
  • Volume dry-up / dry-top divergence

Long-term (200+ days)

  • 200-SMA, golden/death cross
  • Multi-timeframe stack (5/20/50/200)
  • Market regime (SPY vs 200-SMA + 60-bar slope)

Cross-cutting

  • Per-horizon weight tuning across 12 horizons (5d–1260d)
  • 5-fold cross-validation for honest out-of-sample accuracy
  • K-fold WITH sector-aware methodology weighting (the "lift test" panel)
  • Auto-pruning of below-chance methodologies
  • Auto-suppress bearish meta when its accuracy is < 45% (numerical model handles bearish better)
  • Asymmetric bearish quorum (markets drift up; bearish calls need 3+ methodologies vs 2 for bullish)
  • Sector-aware methodology weighting on live signals: Tech uses Tech's best methods, Energy uses Energy's, Utilities mostly silenced
  • Risk-adjusted ranking: top picks match your risk-per-trade volatility profile (2% risk → target ATR ~3% of price)
  • Earnings-date awareness via Yahoo Finance calendar
  • Headline sentiment (VADER) with asymmetric thresholds + cross-sectional relative labels to compensate for VADER's positive bias
  • Live price overlay via Cloudflare Worker proxy (~30s polling during market hours)
  • 9 hourly workflow runs per US market day + after-close + after-hours sweep (~1,584 min/month — under GitHub Actions free tier)

Methodology sources

  • Murphy, "Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets" — SMA crossovers, MACD, support/resistance, trend definitions
  • Nison, "Japanese Candlestick Charting Techniques" — engulfing, hammer, shooting star, doji
  • Wilder, "New Concepts in Technical Trading Systems" (1978) — RSI(14), ATR
  • Bollinger, "Bollinger on Bollinger Bands" — band squeeze + breakout setups
  • Jegadeesh & Titman (1993) — relative strength / momentum factor
  • Stacking ensembles — standard ML practice; the meta-ensemble weights each sub-method by its k-fold out-of-sample accuracy
  • Methodology auto-pruning — drop below-chance approaches before they pollute the ensemble (standard ML hygiene)
